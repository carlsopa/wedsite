Our story

Meet the happy couple

Paul Carlson

Paul Robert Carlson is a retail manager at the Lidl grocery stores and freelance programmer. He was born in Minnesota, went to college for archeology in South Dakota, later moving to Midland, Michigan and Detroit, and eventually Washington DC. He worked at Kmart for close to ten years, a career that runs in the family as his father and uncle worked for Kmart stores as well. His hobbies include balloon twisting and performing magic, as well as tinkering with electronics and anything on the computer. He and Michelle both share a love of technology, hiking, sci-fi fantasy and musical theater.

Michelle Minkoff

Michelle Elyse Minkoff is a data journalist at the Associated Press, meaning she uses technology and computer programming to tell stories in the news. She's worked as an adjunct professor for Northwestern, and given talks in the field across the United States. She was born and raised in the Illinois suburbs. She went to college in Massachusetts and graduate school at Northwestern in Evanston, Ill, and Washington DC. Her hobbies include arts and crafts, reading everything she can get her hands on, cooking, yoga and hiking.

How we met

Our love story began on the Internet (which we can both credit with many great things in our lives). We had both joined match.com about the same time, and were interested in each others' profiles, both featuring computer nerdery and reference to obscure musical theater and fantasy novels.


After about a week of messaging, we agreed to meet at the Newseum. DC protests prevented us from meeting there but we relocated and spent the whole day lunching, exploring historic homes and referencing Doctor Who.


One date led to many -- as we explored comic book stores, museums, Great Falls Park and more. Captivated by each others' passion, intellect and quirkiness, we soon were texting and chatting most nights.


In March 2021, Paul and Michelle went on a trip to Chicago to visit Michelle's mom. While there, the couple went to a dinner at Michelle's cousins' house. After the meal, Paul explained how much family meant to him, then said he would be honored to join theirs, and asked Michelle to marry him! She of course said (screamed) yes! Unbeknownst to Michelle, Paul had sneakily asked Ruth and his parents for their blessing earlier that day.


We both love to travel and have taken day trips and hiking excursions through the DC area, as well as longer trips to Colorado, Texas, Illinois and Michigan. We have dreams of much more national and international travel. Sharing and discussing experiences, exchanging our knowledge of different lifestyles as well as the cultural and culinary arts, we are each others' favorite travel partners.


We have been with each other through job changes, deaths, and hospital visits. Each experience has helped us grow stronger as a couple and fall deeper in love.


We can't wait to commit to each other in the eyes of both our religions and cultures and make a lifetime promise to each other. We are delighted to share this time with you as we rejoice in our love and our lives together!

Accomodations

Near Starved Rock State Park, there are a variety of accomodation options. Starved Rock Lodge itself has rooms and cabins available, as does its' neigboring lodge, Great Bear Lodge, a short drive from our venue. Nearby towns have plenty of the chains you'd expect, including Best Western, Quality Inn and more. There are some, but limited, AirBnBs in the area, but we encourage you to book early if you are interested. For Starved Rock Lodge and Great Bear Resort, as well as two other chain hotels, we are working on getting room blocks for guests of our wedding.
